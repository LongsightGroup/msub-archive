Wiki

The Wiki tool allows your site participants to create and maintain a wiki (that is, a web site that allows groups of people to create and change its content without possessing any web programming skills or technical knowledge). Instead of using programming commands to format text, create new pages, insert pictures and links, etc., users insert special punctuation that is automatically converted into appropriate programming commands. Users can edit and save wiki pages and see the results immediately.

The content for a wiki comes from its users. Everyone that uses it can contribute and change it as they choose. As a result, a wiki tends to change over time to suit the changing needs of its users.

Wikis have some unique features that offer new options to educators. One is that wikis can be accessed from any computer with internet access. This makes them well suited to group activities that take place over time. Another feature of a wiki is that it is not simply a current snapshot of a group’s work. Instead, it saves all previous versions of the work, along with historical information on who made what changes when. As a result, contributions can be tracked. Also, users can revert to a previous version of any page at any time so work is never lost, even if it has been defaced or destroyed. Finally, Wiki pages are not necessarily public. They are available only to members of your site unless you choose to make them fully public.
