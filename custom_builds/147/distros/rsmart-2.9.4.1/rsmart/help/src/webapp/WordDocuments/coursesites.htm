Introducing the CLE

Course Sites

Course sites are designed to facilitate teaching and learning online. They may be used to support a variety of different activities, from passive posting of material, such as a syllabus, to working actively with students in a blended learning situation, to the hosting of fully online, virtual classes.

Key features of course sites include:

•      Course sites allow instructors to create a space to offer online virtual classes, give tests and quizzes, post grades, and facilitate students' learning experiences.

•      Student can submit assignments, view grades, and receive feedback from an instructor.

•      Instructors and students can engage in online discussion and collaboration, using discussion forums, chat rooms, and wiki tools.

Each course site is owned by a user who is given a role (such as instructor or teaching assistant) that allows him or her to populate content and manage the site, create assessment instruments, and establish communication with students. The site owner can also specify who can access and modify the site. Course sites may be open to the public, but in most cases, or access is restricted to specific students in a course.

If you are an instructor, you must consider a number of different factors in designing and setting up a course site. You may want to start by asking yourself a few questions, such as:

•      What pedagogical approach do I plan to use?

•      How large is my class?

•      What forms of assessment will I need?

•      Do I want to use groups and/or sections?

•      Do I plan to incorporate portfolio type activity into my instruction?

Depending on how you answer these questions, you may choose to limit the tools and functionality on your site to the basics, or you may capitalize on the opportunity for innovative teaching and learning and use the full set of tools available. The choice is yours.
