Specifying Settings and Dates

You may specify settings and dates for an evaluation at either of two points:

•      As step two of creating a new evaluation (see Creating Evaluations), immediately after completing step one (see Adding the Evaluation), or

•      any time within 5 calendar days of exiting the creation process without completing the evaluation. In this case, you access the evaluation by clicking the Continue creating this evaluation link for it in the Partially Complete Evaluations section of the My Evaluations screen. 

Warning: If you do not complete a partial evaluation within 5 calendar days, the system deletes it.

In either case, the system displays the Evaluation Settings screen.

Proceed as follows:

1.  In the Evaluation Title section, make any changes you like:

•      Edit the evaluation’s title and/or instructions by changing the entries in those fields.

•      View the selected template by clicking Preview and closing the window to return to this screen.

•      Change the template by clicking Edit and proceeding as directed in Editing Templates, beginning with step 4.

Note: When you edit the template in an evaluation, you are changing only a copy of the template (the copy used in this evaluation). The system does not apply these changes to the “master” template (the template you see on the My Templates screen and use to create additional evaluations). To change the “master” template, proceed as directed in Editing Templates.

2.  In the Evaluation Dates section, enter appropriate start and end dates for this evaluation or select dates via the calendar icons. Your institution may also ask that you provide a separate stop date to allow users a grace period beyond the due date. Users cannot access the evaluation before the start date or after the end date.

3.  In the Evaluation Results section:

a.  Specify who can view the evaluation results after the evaluation has closed. The options are as follows:

o  Private (only you may see the results)

o  Configurable (view options are pre-set by system administrator)

o  Public (all users may see the results)

b.  If the screen contains a View Date field, specify the date on which results are to be made available. If you do not have or use this field, the evaluation is automatically made viewable on its due date. Review the default settings established by your system administrator. If any of these settings need to be changed, contact the administrator.

4.  In the Participant Completion Settings section, review the default settings established by your system administrator. If any of these settings need to be changed, contact the administrator.

5.  In the Administrative Settings section, use the dropdown list to specify whether users need to log in to participate in the evaluation. The options are:

•      Central Authentication Required (only CLE users may participate in this evaluation, and they must log into the CLE to do so)

Important: If you choose Central Authentication Required, all evaluators must log into the CLE in order to take this evaluation. Users who do not have a CLE username and password and do not belong to an evaluator group will not be able to access the evaluation.

•      None (anyone with access to the evaluation’s URL may participate in the evaluation, and they need not log into the CLE to do so)

6.  In the Evaluations Notification section:

a.  To view the notification or reminder email sent to participants, click the appropriate View/Edit… email? link. For information on changing the content of these emails, see Changing Notification Emails.

b.  Select from the dropdown list to specify how frequently reminder emails are to be sent to evaluators who have not yet responded.

c.  In the Email From/Reply-to Address field, enter the email address that will be used as both the “from” and “reply to” addresses in these emails.

7.  To continue, click Continue to Assign to Courses. The CLE displays the Edit Evaluation Assignment screen. (Or, to return to the previous screen without saving your work here, click Cancel.)

8.  To proceed to the next step in the overall process, see Assigning Groups of Evaluators.
