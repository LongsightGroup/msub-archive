Accessing the Tests & Quizzes Tool

To access the Tests & Quizzes tool, click Tests & Quizzes in the tools menu on the left side of your screen. The CLE displays the Tests & Quizzes home page.

If you are an instructor, you will see a home page similar to what is shown above. The home page is also known as the Assessments page. On this page, you’ll see two tabs, each with a different list of assessments:

•      Working Copies are the assessments you have created, but in unpublished form. You may change anything in an unpublished assessment.

•      Published Copies are the assessments you have published. A published assessment that is open to students will have an active status, while a published assessment that is not currently available will have an inactive status.

When you click the Published Copies tab, you can filter your view of published assessments by clicking the Active or Inactive link, and you can also view both active and inactive assessments by clicking the All link. If you edit an active published assessment, the CLE retracts it. The assessment will be listed as inactive, and you will have to republish it to make it active again.

Note: Since a published assessment is a copy of the unpublished version, editing the unpublished version will not change the published copy.

The lists of both unpublished and published assessments include the title, author, and date/time of last modification for each assessment. The list of published assessments also includes the following columns of information:

•      In Progress indicates how many students have opened but not yet submitted each assessment.

•      Submitted indicates the number of students who have submitted each assessment; click on this number to add or edit scores for an assessment.

•      Release To indicates whether an assessment is available to all site members, only to specific groups, or to anonymous users, so CLE login is not required for access.

•      Release Date indicates when each assessment is open to students.

•      Due date indicates the due date for each assessment; depending on an assessment’s settings, students may not be able to submit after this date, or they may be allowed to submit but their submissions will be marked as late.
