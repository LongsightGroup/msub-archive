Introducing the CLE

Types of Sites in the CLE

Your institution’s CLE contains many web sites.

•      Only one of these—a site called the gateway—is public. Anyone can access your institution’s gateway without entering a user ID or password. From the gateway, they can access items on your site that you have chosen to make public, but they must log in to see items that you have restricted to site members and guests. The gateway may also include various tools that are accessible without a login.

•      Another type of site is completely private. This site is called My Workspace. Each user of the CLE has his or her own private My Workspace site. After you log in, the CLE displays your My Workspace site.

•      All of the other sites in the CLE can have one or many members. These sites are known as group sites. Only users with authorized user IDs and passwords can access them.

There are four types of group sites in the CLE:

•      Course sites (course management)

•      Project sites (project collaboration)

•      Portfolio sites (creating, viewing, and evaluating ePortfolios)

•      Administration (administering the CLE at your institution)

The first three types of sites are intended for end users, such as instructors, students, project organizers, and participants. Administration sites are intended for Sakai CLE Administrators only. This documentation covers sites for end users – course, project, and portfolio sites.
