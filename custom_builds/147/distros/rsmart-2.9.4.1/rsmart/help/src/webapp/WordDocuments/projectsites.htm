Introducing the CLE

Project Sites

Project sites are designed to facilitate online collaboration. They may be used to support a variety of activities, from students working on class projects to scientists collaborating on a grant proposal, a tenure committee evaluating faculty work, and administrators revising the undergraduate curriculum.

Key features of project sites include:

•      Project sites allow a project organizer to post information about a project for group members, schedule meetings, and send announcements.

•      Project participants use the site to engage in collaborative work, share data, and participate in synchronous and asynchronous discussions about their work.

Each project site is owned by a user who is given a role (such as Organizer) that allows him or her to add content, control site membership, manage the site, and establish communication with participants. The site owner can also specify who can access and modify the site: Project sites may be open to the public or access may be limited to specific groups.

If you are responsible for a project of some sort, you must consider a number of different factors in designing and setting up a project site. You may want to start by asking yourself these questions:

•      What is the objective of the project?

•      How do site participants need to interact with each other?

•      What tools are needed to support this interaction?

•      What roles do my site participants play?

Depending on your answers to these questions, you may wish to limit the tools and functionality on your site to the basics of posting and exchanging information with site participants. Alternatively, you may wish to offer a more robust set of tools that enable full collaboration and sharing on your site. The choice is yours.
