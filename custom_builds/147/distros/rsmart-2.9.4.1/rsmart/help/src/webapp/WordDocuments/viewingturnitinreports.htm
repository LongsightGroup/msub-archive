Viewing Turnitin Reports

To view Turnitin originality reports for an assignment, access the Assignments tool and follow these steps:

1.  On the Assignments home page, find the assignment you want to check. If the assignment will be graded, click the Grade link. If it has no grade scale, click the View Submissions link. In either case, the CLE displays the Submissions screen for the assignment. On this screen, you’ll see an additional Turnitin column.

2.  In this column, view the Turnitin icon () for each student. The color of the icon indicates the “similarity index,” which is the percentage of text in the submission that matches or is similar to content in the Turnitin database:

•      blue - no matching text

•      green - one word to 24%

•      yellow – 25%-49%

•      orange – 50%-74%

•      red -75%-100%

If an originality report has not yet been completed, you’ll see "no report" in the Turnitin column.

Note: The speed of processing for Turnitin originality reports will depend on the job schedule set by your CLE administrator.  

3.  Click on an icon to access the originality report for that student in the Turnitin Document Viewer. 

The document viewer provides a display of the submission. In the viewer, you can:

•      Determine which portions of the submission match other content.

•      View the source of the matching content. 

•      Find more information about submission matches in the Match Overview box on the right side of the viewer.

•      Access a resubmission from the student, by selecting the appropriate submission from the dropdown menu in the viewer’s upper right corner.

•      Access the student’s submission for another assignment posted in your site, by selecting the appropriate assignment from the dropdown menu in the viewer’s upper left corner.

Keep in mind that the content matches you see will depend on your selections for the Check originality against setting, as instructed in Setting up Turnitin. For more information about viewing originality reports for submissions, see Viewing Originality Reports on the Turnitin web site.
