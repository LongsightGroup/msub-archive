Certification

If you’re an instructor or site owner, the Certification tool lets you provide certificates that students may earn for a variety of achievements, such as mastery of course material or completion of a program. You set gradebook criteria that students must meet to print a certificate, in PDF format. Certificates can be individualized for each student. The Certification tool is usually available in course sites.

Note: To use the Certification tool, you will need a PDF template with form fields.

When using this tool, the typical workflow for providing a certificate is the following:

1.  Upload a PDF template.

2.  Enter values for the fields in the template. These values are used to populate the fields when students print the certificate.

3.  Define the criteria for printing the certificate, by adding “rules” (conditions) for gradebook scores/settings in Gradebook/Gradebook2.

4.  Activate the certificate.

Once the certificate is published, students can access the Certification tool and click on a "status" link for the certificate. If they have met all criteria, they will be able to print the certificate. Otherwise, the CLE displays the criteria that have not been met. An instructor may also provide students with a “status” URL for a certificate. When students click on this link, they are taken to the tool, and the CLE indicates whether the criteria for printing the certificate have been met.
