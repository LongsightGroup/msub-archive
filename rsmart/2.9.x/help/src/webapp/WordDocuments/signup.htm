Sign-up

The Sign-up tool lets site owners schedule, and other site participants sign up for, a variety of events, such as office hour visits, review sessions, and study groups. This tool is typically added to course and project sites. Key features and functions of the tool include:

•      Multiple meeting types—site owners may create a meeting with a single time period; a multiple-slot meeting, which can be divided into time slots of equal duration or customized slots of any duration; or an open meeting that is one time period with no sign-up required.

•      Assigning options—site owners may opt to let users sign up for meetings or assign users to meetings.

•      Availability—site owners may make a meeting available to all participants or to selected groups, as well as to participants in other sites. Site owners may determine how often a meeting occurs, and they may also set a meeting’s sign-up time period.

•      Automatic group creation—site owners may opt to have groups automatically generated for the time slots of a meeting. The groups are then populated according to the site members who sign up or are assigned to the meeting.

•      Attendance—site owners may track attendance of meetings.

•      Assign meeting organizers and coordinators—a site owner may opt to make another site member the organizer of a meeting, as well as add site members as coordinators to manage a meeting. (Note: site members must have the appropriate permissions to be meeting organizers or coordinators.)

•      Rich text editor--both site owners and site participants (if allowed by a site owner) have access to the rich text editor. A site owner may include a description when adding a meeting, while site participants (if allowed) may include a comment when signing up for a meeting.

•      Meeting management—site owners may edit, copy, remove, and print meetings, as well as export meeting information as Excel spreadsheet files. Site owners may also re-assign users to different time slots, as well as lock specific time slots so no users may sign up for them. Meeting attendees may print or export meetings too, but export files contain limited information.

•      Notifications—site owners may opt to notify site participants when a meeting has been published or changed, and may also opt to have a reminder email sent a day in advance of a meeting. In addition, site owners may opt to receive email notification when a participant signs up for, or cancels, a meeting.

•      Calendar tool integration—site owners may opt to have meetings automatically posted in the Calendar tool.

•      iCal attachment—both meeting organizers and attendees may download a meeting in iCal format, so the meeting can be added to an external calendar, such as Microsoft Outlook or Google Calendar.

The tool also provides many other management capabilities: site owners may opt to let site participants see the names of other participants in a meeting; allow participants to sign up for more than one time slot; provide participants with the option to join a wait list for a time slot that is already full; allow or deny site participants the ability to add a comment when signing up; and require site participants to enter a user ID when signing up for a meeting.
