Navigating to a Site

You can navigate to a site in several different ways:

•      Go to My Workspace by clicking My Workspace at the top of the screen.

•      Go to a site that has a site link displayed at the top of the screen, by clicking on the appropriate link.

•      Go to a site that does not have a site link displayed, by accessing a full list of your active sites.

Tip: You can always access all sites to which you belong (if they are sites you own, both published and unpublished) on the home page of the Site Setup tool.

Depending on how your CLE instance is configured, you can access your full list of active sites in one of two ways:

•      Click the My Sites tab in the upper right corner of the screen. The CLE displays a dropdown menu listing all the active course, portfolio, and project sites to which you belong.   

For a display of hidden sites, click on the View hidden sites link. Go to a site by clicking on the appropriate link.  

•      Click on More Sites, which displays to the right of your site links at the top of the screen. The sites drawer opens.

You’ll see all of the active sites to which you belong, organized by site type and, for course sites, by academic term. Go to a site by clicking on the appropriate site button. 

In the sites drawer, you also have other navigation options:

•      Go directly to a specific tool in a site by clicking the “arrow” icon () for that site, as explained in Navigating to a Tool. 

•      Go directly to the Site Setup tool in My Workspace. Click View All Sites for the tool’s home page, or click +Add New Site for the tool’s Site Type page (note: to add a new site, you’ll need the appropriate permission).

•      Go directly to the Customize Tabs page for the Preferences tool in My Workspace, by clicking Preferences.

To search for active sites, enter keywords in the Search sites in this drawer box. To close the sites drawer, click the red “x” icon.  

Note: Hidden sites will not be displayed in the sites drawer, and you’ll only see the More Sites tab if you have opted to display fewer site links than your total number of active sites. For more information, see Changing Your Site Links, Hiding Sites, and Activating Hidden Sites.
